Quote from: Jean-Luc on November 20, 2014, 11:25:05 am
Some of the proposals do not express well what exactly the forging process is doing. It is not about confirming or validating transactions. Yes, the forger also does this, but so does everybody else, and nobody blindly trusts the forger that the transactions in the forged block are valid, everybody re-validates them before accepting the block. What the forger does is create a new block, and he has the freedom to select which transactions will go into that block, and after the 1.4 hard fork even the order in which the transaction in the block will be executed. So it is about creating/generating/composing/forging/casting a block, and selecting/picking/choosing which transactions to be in that block. Words like confirm, validate, certify, just don't express what is happening. The securing of the network part comes from the fact that the forger is using his stake to create a new block at the time he becomes eligible to do it, rather than skipping his turn which would allow somebody with a smaller stake to create a block some time later (which would lead to a chain with a smaller difficulty, more vulnerable to attacks). But the forger doesn't do any extra processing or validation that others don't.

In the code I just call the function "generateBlock". Maybe casting, to keep the forging theme, or "blockcasting", if we want to invent a new word?
