If you pick the midquote on both side, how do you arrive to the exact right quantity of  the counter asset?
If I want to buy Asset1/Asset2 at 100, and Asset1/NXT best bid ask is (900,1300) and Asset2/NXT best bid ask is (8,12), trading at the midquote means I would have to sell 100x Asset2/NXT at 10 = 1000 NXT and buy 1x Asset1/NXT at 1100, which would let me with 1x Asset1 and -100 NXT. The counterpart node will have got 100 Asset2 and will be long an extra 100 NXT. That doesn't work. If you pick the midquote on one leg, you need to  determine the trading price on the other leg based on the target quantity. Say we picked the midquote P1=10 for the first leg so I sold Q1=100 Asset 2 for 1000 NXT, now the price for the second leg would have to be P2 = P1*Q1/Q2=10*100/1=1000.

This illustrates pretty well one use case of how an attacker could benefit from the edge case discussed earlier. Whatever price you pick for settlement, it will have to be between the best bid and ask on each pair otherwise your transactions would end up taking some passive orders in the AE book. So the price at which the victim will sell her asset on the first leg will always be a better / more agressive price than that of the best ask on AE. The attacker can bait the victim with the hope that she can close the complete cross-asset transaction at the expected price, but turn around after the first leg has been executed so that the victim ends up having sold her asset for half a spread cheaper than the best ask price.

Actually, there is even a way to game that even harder: if you use the midquote without taking into account the quantities, the attacker just needs to post a tiny order to move the best ask of the first pair much lower just before starting the attack. This will have the effect of skewing the midquote heavily on the left side and make an even better bargain for the attacker. To avoid that you need to look at the quantities, but then there are other strategies to game the calculation of the midquote (I can detail if you want).

The point is: the more you try to prevent people gaming the system, the more attack surface you are creating and the more opportunities and corner cases there will be to game the system. Atomic transactions are a must. Any work around will just open a vulnerability somewhere else.
