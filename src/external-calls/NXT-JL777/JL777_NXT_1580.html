General / Re: New Powerful Attacks On ECDSA
« on: October 29, 2014, 08:39:40 am »
Quote from: Zahlen on October 29, 2014, 07:10:59 am
Extracting 3 questions from the previous post for visibility. Could a dev reply? I'd like to add their answers to the wiki.

1) How is the private key derived from your passphrase?

2) How are signing keys for each transaction derived from the tx data and your private key?

3) How does Nxt Wallet/Wesley's UI generate the suggested passphrases?
I only know the answer to 1)

In C:
Code: [Select]
   static uint8_t basepoint[32] = {9};
    uint64_t addr;
    uint8_t hash[32];
    calc_sha256(0,mysecret,(unsigned char *)pass,(int32_t)strlen(pass));
    mysecret[0] &= 248;
    mysecret[31] &= 127;
    mysecret[31] |= 64;
    curve25519_donna(mypublic,mysecret,basepoint);
    calc_sha256(0,hash,mypublic,32);
    memcpy(&addr,hash,sizeof(addr));
    return(addr);
SHA256(passphrase) mapped to valid privkeys by setting and clearing a few bits. That's the passphrase -> private key.

For completeness, after that the privatekey and the basepoint are curve25519 -> public key

Then to get the NXT addr, SHA256 the public key and take the low 64 bits
