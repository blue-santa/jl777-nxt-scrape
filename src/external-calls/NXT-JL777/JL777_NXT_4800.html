This is a common practice for a conglomerate,especially in Japan.
Interlinked ownership stabilizes valuations as even in the worst case if one company isnt doing so well, it will at least still have value derived from owning the other company's shares

Now, jl777hodl is simple a holding company of all the other assets, plus I toss in bonus assets from third parties when I can. This is not an operating company and is meant to allow people to be able to make one purchase that is a play on NXT in general succeeding.

NXTventure owning 20% of itself is fairly unique and this is so that NXTventure asset holders will benefit not only from the monthly dividends, but also from long term appreciation of the NXTventure issues.

I am not trying to confuse, just trying to create a stable structure of interlinked operating companies with holding companies to offer convenient methods of horizontal ownership.

Maybe the common ownership of sharkfund? That is explained by the efficiency gained from having a consolidated account for short term positions. The operating companies dont need much NXT to keep operating and rather than fragmenting the funds across many accts, it is much easier to manage from one, plus a large total balance allows for the possibility of some unique short term opportunities that might not be available otherwise

Generally speaking I like to encapsulate one market segment into a single asset. That allows people to invest in just that area. So operating companies like InstantDEX, NXTautoDAC, MGW, NXTcoinsco, etc dont have much cross ownerships. NXTventure is a special case due to its special function.
