meaning, can they be trusted?

I think the goal is that there will be many different companies running OT servers and will form a group(a voting pool). Let's say there are 100 different OT operators, each node is one signature of a 20 of 100 multisig wallet. This would prevent a single operator, or even a small group of operators from getting together and stealing the funds since you would need 20 votes. During this time the owner of the BTC can trade and use their btc within the system, off chain. They can move from person to person with high frequency, by updating a database record shared among nodes and when a user wishes to withdraw the coins they would request a vote from 20 computers by proving that they own the last signed receipt for the coins. I believe the goal is that well known trusted entities such as exchanges, would operate these servers and all of them would have a shared interest in ensuring that nobody in their pool tries to rip anybody off since it would destroy all of their businesses together. People wouldn't be allowed to join this pool unless they were deemed trustworthy by the rest of the pool. This technology can be used so that you no longer have to trust a single website or exchange with your money and thus eliminating that single point of failure, this includes hacking, loss, theft etc.... and allows for cheap micropayments, high frequency trading and other benefits that a server can provide that blockchain technology can't. It's basically extremely advanced multisig.
