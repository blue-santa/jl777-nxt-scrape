I could be missing something, buy "trimming" blockchains seems a bit unfair to old, accounts of long term hodlers.  I've not studied up on the finite details of the newer, anonymous currencies, but all current crypto works by forwarding values of old transactions to new accounts.  If you hold an account with a deposit from 2-4 years ago, and the blockchain is trimmed back to 18 months ago, wouldn't you loose your funding?

The only plausible way I see around this is to have some form of re-organization routine that can create new, qausi-valid transactions that consolidate all transactions for these old accounts.  In other words, if an account balance is the summation of 200 transactions, all from 2 years ago, find a way to sum these transaction values and then write them to 1, valid transaction.  I'm not sure how this could ever be possible, because this would require some form of "authority" that could sign these transactions and mark them as valid, even though they aren't signed by the owners of these stale accounts.
it is a matter of having a ledger to compress all the past activity to a set of starting balances. Basically part ripple/part blockchain evil mutant hybrid.
Since all the nodes at any given time (more or less) will agree on balances, theoretically if they all spent the time to synchronize a ledger, it could happen without any central authority. Of course, this is for a simple coin like bitcoin where there are just balances and for something as multifaceted as NXT, well, I will avoid this headache by not volunteering to do this

Jame
