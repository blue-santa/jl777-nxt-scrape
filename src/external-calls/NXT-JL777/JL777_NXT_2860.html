In light of recent events, can we have a think about adding functionality to Nxt that allows veto'ing transactions without the need for rollbacks.

I'm thinking two/three new transaction types.. first a vote to freeze an account.. if 10% of active stake (stake of last 24 hours forgers + stake of any other ppl that vote) if 10% are in favour then the account in question is frozen for 6, 12 or 24 hours..  During this time a second vote is held as to whether to create a rollback transaction or to let it stay.  If 50% (or maybe 70%) or more of active stake (stake of last 24 hours + stake of any other ppl that vote) vote for rollback then a rollback transaction is created by the next forging node to confirm transaction rolled back.

Nxt General Discussion / Re: Veto stolen transactions without rollback
« on: August 16, 2014, 06:18:38 am »
Quote from: slothbag on August 16, 2014, 06:10:14 am
Quote
why rely on the community to act proactively on behalf of you, if you can easily make practically uncrackable cold storage?
My M of N implementation can handle N of up to 254, which is bit overkill, but also allows having 100+ trusted people to hold a fragment who would commit to actually doing something when it is required.

there will be N different keys, so if you lose all N, well you shouldnt be using crypto!

It sounds great, but BTER were using the same password across many systems.. thats security 101 and they failed it.. they probably should fall into the "shouldnt be using crypto" bucket but no one could of known that until the hack came to light.

Quote
If there is ANY chance of blockchain to not be permanent, then I (and everyone else that relies on the blockchain data) will have to add whatever the max period is to the number of confirmations needed. So if your reversible plan allows for reversing a tx up to one day after the event, then I would change MGW wait times to one day.

I agree, it would mean confirmation time would probably go up.. but what is it currently? a re-org can happen up to 720 blocks or something.. if we make the "freeze vote" only applicable < 720 blocks then nothing needs to change.
the 720 is really a backstop. It is very rare for more than 10 blocks to be popped off on a node and the chances go down rapidly the more blocks that go by. I can imagine the screamings if I change the min NXT confirms to 720!
Right now if three different nodes are in agreement and it is 10 blocks deep, odds are very good that it is correct, combined that with there being a critical tx right at the moment of max depth block popping, then the odds become tolerable.

This externally invoked undoing a tx is not something that would follow a statistical model, it would just happen out of the blue even though 100% of nodes sampled all say otherwise.

What you suggest is the wrong path. I see two things that would solve 99% of practical issues. One is reliable and automated cold storage, with a way to have it publicly verified that you are doing this. The other is the problem of sending to the wrong address, a valid one, but accidentally it goes to the wrong account. A solution to that is a simple two step tx, eg. just have the person you want to send funds to send you an "invoice" with info on it to verify it is the correct person, eg. details only they would know like what the tx is for, etc. Then you send the funds by approving the invoice.

With the invoice method and n<=254 cold storage, can you think of any other times that you would even consider undoing a transaction?

James
