Quote from: ShortNXT on May 30, 2014, 06:44:55 pm
Quote from: anon136 on May 30, 2014, 06:39:40 pm
can you give me an ever so brief explanation of how that works? usually short selling involves an investor borrowing instruments, selling the instruments, holding onto the cash for a period of time, rebuying back as many instruments as they can for that amount of cash, and then returning the borrowed instruments to the lender, ideally with extra instruments left over for himself. Is that how shortnxt works or is it something different?

It's a little different, but is designed to offer similar exposure. It works more like an inverse ETF, where the daily percentage change in the value of ShortNXT aims to be the inverse of the daily percentage change in NXT/USD.  The issuer supports this price peg with standing buy and sell orders on the Asset Exchange.  So if NXT/USD falls by 5% on a given day, the ShortNXT target price will rise by 5% that day.

More info is available here and the daily reference prices are documented at http://twitter.com/shortnxt
