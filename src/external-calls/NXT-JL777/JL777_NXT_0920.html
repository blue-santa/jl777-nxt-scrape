Let me try here to give some examples...

Look at the blockchain technology as another type of database system. Very slow to write in it, so not good for a real time system, but amazing for distributed situations, and sealed guaranteed data system. Data writing and reading can be done through a web gateway (or clients without blockchain) so that the data recorded can remain protected.

Take an hotel chain (Accor, for example). They have thousands of hotels all around the world (Ibis, Mecure, Novotel, etc). Each hotel has a server room, and they spend fortunes in the IT infrastructure. Let say each hotel adds a small server that acts as node for their private Nxt blockchain (a sort of private testnet, not something mixed with the blockchain we use daily for our assets and NXT transactions).

Now you have a full stable structure for a private secure data repository. A new type of database that cannot be cheated by a corrupted IT support person. What can be recorded here? What can it be used for?

1) Security audits (from "room access" data to local databases backup hashes archive)
2) Client loyalty points (combined to a mobile app for the hotel clients to be used through a web gateway)
3) Employees assignments/roles and reporting (as a Human Resource distributed system)
4) Secure communication (encrypted communications to employees also combined to a mobile app)
5) Invoices archive (at the moment an hotel emits an invoice it is saved there so nobody cheats the records to steal money)
6) etc (I have a couple of super cool ideas, but I am not sharing them yet lol)

...and all the above can be applied to airlines, restaurant chains, bookshops, clothing, etc.

Furthermore, if we manage to interest some government to the technology, it can be enforced and used for registering certificates, invoices (think a country where to validate an invoice it has to be registered in the blockchain! No more faking the books will be possible, and in an instant all the business relationships are available for analysis to the IRS, and they can see from production to the final user all the price markups applied to a product, and who is cheating the system).

The applications are unlimited. It is a matter of getting started. Once we have a small simple user case the second work will come easier, and so on and so forth. Then Nxt will have a strong and stable reputation.

What is important are the open source contracts where we must be sure that the technology cannot be white labelled, and cannot be sold for money (the service and consulting around it can, but not the code).

If this ever happen, you will be shocked to see how much money come in the direction of this community to get a lot of works done, to get Nxt to be implemented to do this and that, etc.

Roberto

Really looking forward to get started with this!

I throw this here, let me know what you think.

Let say that there is a business. Medium to large business. They sell IT solutions. You know, those consultancy firms often affiliated with SAP, Oracle, SAAS, etc. Those medium to large companies that move millions of dollars in projects for IT infrastructures for large corporations.

Let say this business learns about Nxt and all it can and aim to do. Let say, also, that Nxt technology fits requirements of some of their clients. The blockchain technology apply very well to specific needs, where data once written are permanent, and several functions needed for their organisation find a good solution on what Nxt does.

Now this consultancy firm can offer to their client a well tested (by all of us) system that can be deployed privately (with a separate internal blockchain), that is distributed and decentralised along THEIR NETWORK (and thus can be only accessible from their intranet, for example), that can integrate with their existing infrastructure (with some dedicated coding).

Well, this company would save money (rather than paying licenses to private vendors they use an open source app: Nxt), have a large community that already know the system (they are not adopting a new technology from an unknown new vendor with no user base), the existing community is made mostly by IT savvy people, they have developers that can be hired that know the code quite well...

They will adopt Nxt, finance bounties for development (surely for things they are more interested about, but hey, money flow on us), and help building a strong reputation for our platform (without affecting our precious blockchain).

Now, often the issue is "who do we talk to for Nxt?" It may seem a stupid question, but let me give you a good example: at the beginning of the Internet era a friend of mine was chief of the IT of a worldwide distributed clothing company. Each of their shops, even the one in franchise, were connected to their headquarters via dedicated phone line. The monthly bill was huge. I suggested them to use the internet instead, and they could not proceed that way, as there is not such thing as "Mr Internet" they can go complain if something is not working properly.

I saw that there are efforts to incorporate an Nxt foundation (very important mostly for the above explained reason), but I am wondering why not opening an Nxt, Inc. that actually start selling IT solutions based on the Nxt technology. Free code, paid consulting.

I have the strong belief that implementations of solutions that are used for business reasons will give the grounds to Nxt that no/few other crypto platform has. Nxt has the advantage that is functional NOW.

Who would be in with me for such a venture? And before all, is it an idea that makes sense?
