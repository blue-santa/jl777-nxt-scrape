You will notice that these two transactions are the opposite of each other. The comment field has what each transaction wants in return. Also key is that the second transaction has a referencedTransactionFullHash, which matches the fullHash of the first tx. This is how the two are linked, so the second transaction would wait until the first transaction was seen on the blockchain. In order to give assurance to the other party that the transaction was indeed submitted, not only is the txid sent back, but also the signed tx bytes. this way both parties can broadcast the transaction that they like, eg. the one where they get stuff. I am automating all this so it all just works, but the worst an attacker can do by changing the code is to prevent the broadcast of the first tx or the second tx. If he stops the first one from going out, the second one never happens and will expire. If he stops the second from from going out, well why would he, thats the transaction that gives him his half, so in order to collect on that he has to broadcast his tx.

What does all this mean???

It means InstantDEX can now matchup exact trades and get them to trade nearly atomically. uMGW / Re: uMGW Financial Model And Process
« on: July 24, 2014, 03:01:52 am »
Quote from: cobaltskky on July 24, 2014, 02:29:41 am
So, I'm trying to get a feel for how the fees work with uMGW.  Here are the scenarios that I came up with where the operator accounts are charged a fee(please correct me if I'm wrong):

Customer requests deposit address: 2 NXT ( heard it was 2, but can't recall why 2 instead of just 1)
Customer deposits coins to account: 1 NXT

Then when the customer withdraws coins from uMGW, they are charged:

Coin blockchain fee
Multigateway fee

So, to make this sustainable and profitable for operators, we need to make sure that the Multigateway withdraw fee is enough to cover our customers generating a deposit address(one time fee per customer to operators) and our customers depositing coin (ongoing fee to operators).  It also has to include the cost of platform overhead(~$40 per server at the moment with AWS).  We've also mentioned that, I believe, the community fund and/or multigateway fund gets a large cut of the fee - like 50% to 30% I believe?  And we've mentioned setting up an insurance fund in case of an attack by a malicious agent or irrecoverable data loss.  This quickly adds up, eh?

Based on this information, we'll need to set our fees accordingly.  The fee will need to be high enough to both cover our costs and(in a way) dissuade customers from withdrawing capital from the NXT ecosystem.  However, the fee can't be outrageously high, or we won't be able to "sell" the idea of using the multigateway.

Also, I'll be honest about the fact that I want to profit from this venture. lol  I don't want to price gouge, but I do want to make money.  So, a fee that "breaks even" doesn't appeal to me.  I would prefer to receive a small amount of revenue per withdraw, and make up for the "smallness" of the revenue by a large volume.

It may behoove us to start with a break even fee, and then slowly ramp it up to a profitable, target price over time.  That way we can keep the barrier for entry low while still covering our costs(and slowly building an insurance fund).  I think we need that barrier for entry to be low for new customers to enter the Multigateway/NXT exchange.  Once we reach a certain threshhold of regular usage, then we can start increasing the fee to provide reward for our operators for their hard work and assuming some of the risk.

I'm not going to suggest how much the fee should be at the moment.  I guess I just wanted us to get thinking about the fee and to start the conversation.
To send AM with deposit address costs 1 NXT
To do asset transfer in response to each deposit costs 1 NXT
The withdraw is charged coin's (txfee + NXTfee_equiv)

A key point is that when volumes go up and batch mode starts doing more than one withdraw at a time, MGW starts spitting out extra NXTfee_equiv as profits. It is breakeven for a round trip, deposit+withdraw assuming it is the only withdraw in the batch.
There is actually no cost incurred by MGW in receiving the withdraw request, that xfer cost is already paid by the user, so the way I look at it that we are deferring the cost of sending the asset transfer triggered by deposit until the withdraw.

Now, maybe the average deposit size wont match average withdraw size and this model implicitly assumes roughly the same number of deposits and withdraws. For uMGW to be making money, it wont be from the withdraw fees.

I am building in MGW monetization from a revenue sharing with InstantDEX. InstantDEX will be supporting tradebots, active traders and will charge 0.1%, so a share of this is an order of magnitude more than any withdraw fee you can get away with charging.

As long as we can run the MGW on a breakeven basis, it will get a lot of deposits. The more deposits the more trades. Possibly 100 trades per withdraw. So please follow my plan. We get marketshare and view enabling asset trading via AE as a public service. The money is made from active traders using InstantDEX

James
