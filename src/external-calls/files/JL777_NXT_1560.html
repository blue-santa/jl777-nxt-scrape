1579  Alternate cryptocurrencies / Announcements (Altcoins) / Re: [ANN]BitNet-vpncoin DARKNET|AES|Domain&Website System|VPN|Voice&video call on: December 13, 2014, 06:58:09 AM
to spend bitcoin, you need to hash your privkey and it needs to match the pubkey, since the hashing functions generate high entropy outputs, the odds of the output being all 0 is 1^256th power and for bitcoin there is a second stage hashing, so it is even more remote. Not a formal proof, but good enough for me.

Basically when you do an SHA256, there will be a distribution of 1 bits and 0 bits, with 128 of each being the most common, but 127 and 129 are possible at about half the chance of the prior count. so the odds are vanishing small, that anybody can ever find a privkey that SHA256 to 0. Now bitcoin uses RIPEMD160, which is 2 stages of hashing, so not a 0 output from SHA256, but an output that in turn generates a 0 output.

tl:dr There isnt any greater risk of someone being able to spend the burnt coins than they being able to spend any address. In fact, due to the all zero nature the odds are much, much, much less chance to crack this address.
