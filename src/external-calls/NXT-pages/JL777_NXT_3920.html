SuperNET Releases / Re: [BETA M08g5] How To Use Multigateway with NXTservices: A Test User Guide
« on: June 23, 2014, 11:31:18 pm »
Quote from: msin on June 23, 2014, 11:15:02 pm
Quote from: jl777 on June 23, 2014, 10:28:40 pm
a) no centralized exchange I know of allows you to monitor unspent BTC outputs in realtime, MGW does
b) no centralized exchange I know of publishes source code to their automated deposit/withdraw handling, MGW will and already does to trusted members
c) no centralized exchange implements independent party multisig like MGW does

All 3 are great and will definitely help ease concerns.  From a user perspective, the biggest concern I see is a hacker finding a way to withdrawal more BTC than they own in AE.  The layman user will be nervous that their BTC funds are secure while trading on AE.  I do think many users who acquire MGWBTC will probably withdrawal immediately for cold storage purposes (which will be good for NxtServices fees), especially because it's so easy to deposit BTC into MGW and AE.  I guess if you have concerns or issues down the road, you could always have a limit on BTC withdrawal amounts per user.
All the MGW servers (and however many MGW clients) are calculating the current AE balance for any account that is doing withdraws. I do have a check in there to make sure both the overall MGW virtual wallet has enough funds.

The withdrawal is done by transferring the asset, so to do a withdraw you had to have had the asset to transfer. Now, the recent GLEFU incident stressed the importance of making sure the blockchain is happy and I am not satisfied with the wait for N blocks method as a node could go on a fork that is > N blocks long. Of course, the odds of all three MGW servers all going on their own >N blocks fork is remote and for them to all still agree on much of anything is probably even more unlikely.

The default response to anything strange is that withdraws get stuck, so that feels right.

I am working out the details for dynamically verifying the state of the NXT network (aka NXT health monitor) and will build that into MGW's required confirmations.

Once that is in place, I cannot see how anybody can transfer more assets than they have. If anybody does, please speak up.

James
