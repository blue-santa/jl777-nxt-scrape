Question on how can we put "International Letters of Credit" into NXT? These are letters used to enable a seller to ship goods because a 3rd party has promised that the buyer can pay and is guaranteeing payment...

This is an expensive and slow process at the moment and this company is looking at how something like NXT might be able to help improve this process.

Basically as I understand it this is escrowed funds for international delivery of goods, may not need to do the funds escrow but assist the management of the letter of credit token to automate the release of the funds on delivery between the buyer and the seller.

Tbh this one had me stumped a bit and still does - any ideas?
So we have Seller that gets a letter of credit from guarantor and the buyer who probably secured the payment with guarantor.

The seller waits until guarantor attests that if they make shipment, they are guaranteed to get payment
Now probably the buyer will need to approve the proper receipt of the shipment at a location where it does not control the shipment.
Then if they reject the shipment, buyer doesnt get delivery, everyone is cranky but no money changes hands
If the buyer accepts, they indicate this to the guarantor somehow and they are able to take delivery and the seller gets payment

If I got that right, are they trying to eliminate the guarantor?
We would still need a place where the buyer can inspect the shipment and approve or disapprove the shipment. I guess that is more of a warehousing thing, so bankers arent involved and let us assume we have this.

this might work:
buyer creates an AM message that says they approve the shipment, they dont broadcast
they also create a payment tx to seller that refers to the AM txid

they send to seller both raw tx bytes, the first one unsigned the second one signed
the seller verifies the payment is acceptable and publishes an AM to that effect and broadcasts the payment tx
when the buyer sees the AM, they broadcast the acceptance AM

The warehouse waits for both AMs to appear, the buyers acceptance AM and the sellers approval of payment AM
the warehouse needs to verify that the AM;s havent been altered and if both buyer and seller AMs are received the warehouse releases the shipment

See, pretty simple
Clearly all parties will need to have all this automated and GUI'ized. NXTatomic already implements the buyer and seller portion or at least pretty close, but the warehouse part and using AM's is not there yet. If my algo is good, that means there is nothing stopping this from being done, other than my lack of time

No I need the data to calculate the dividends each asset holder should receive. And need the second script to pay it.
Are you saying you need to track the exact asset across ownership changes and allocate different amounts to each specific asset? If so, that is not possible as assets are fungible. what happens if someone buys asset on Monday and another person buys an asset on Wed, then on thursday they both transfer it to a third person, who then sends half of it to a fourth person.

Please tell me how to allocate the Monday asset and Wed asset between the third person and fourth person.
This is like mixing. You cant use a fungible asset to represent nonfungible things. The Monday asset is not fungible with the Wed asset, yet the NXT assets are all exactly the same. There are no txin/txout threads to backtrace so any allocation will be a statistical allocation.

Might I suggest an alternate approach that has a verifiable solution?

For each dividend period calculate the total dividend you are to issue. You can do this from your sales records, yes? So we have Total_dividend. Then separately we find all the asset holders and add up how many assets are in accounts that are not the issuing acct. Now we can calculate the amount each asset should get by dividing Total_dividend by the sum of assets in the wild. From there its easy to allocate to each account.

Now, you will notice that people who buy the asset right before the dividend date would dilute the dividends from the people who had it all week. Since everyone can do this, and it only happens the first time the asset is issued, it is hopefully an acceptable compromise. If not we are back to a statistical allocation that is a lot more difficult to implement and prone to bugs

If it is a big problem for the edge case of late purchasers, I suggest taking the asset off the market during the last days before the dividend block
