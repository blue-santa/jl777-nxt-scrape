I don't know what is common, you can check the logs for block pop-off messages. The hardcoded limit for switching between forks is 720 blocks, but that's only when a node compares its blockchain to that of the peer it is feeding from currently. It can happen that you talk to a peer on a fork with the common block 700 blocks behind and switch to that one, then see another peer on yet another fork with common block 600 blocks behind and switch to that other one, and overall make more than 720 blocks change, and keep doing that, but the chance of that many forks, with better cumulative difficulty than yours, is really low. And when choosing forks, it is not height but cumulative difficulty that matters - so you can switch to a fork of a lower height than your current height.
Its not very practical to wait for 720 confirmations for MGW.
Any advice on how to be able to trust the most recent blocks?
For instance, if 100 random nodes all agree on the current height and block, is that good enough to trust that it wont reorganize?
