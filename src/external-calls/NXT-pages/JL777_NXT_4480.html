I've been around NXT for a short 6 weeks and I've so missed a lot of the politics that's shaped the community up to this point (I never had much of a head for it anyway, so it's probably as well). It is worth saying that I've been consistently impressed with the talent and motivation of the guys who make things happen here - whether tech, marketing or whatever - and I've got the highest regard for the ones I've worked with directly.
In my offline life I used to be a counsellor for many years, and in a separate strand I ended up doing a lot of work on organisational relationships of all kinds - inter, intra, individual/corporate, etc, etc. I can say that hands down, the biggest predictor of success in any organisation is the quality of its relationships. Think of any success story, and I pretty much guarantee you it's not the tech or ideas alone that does it. Any major failure too. It's the relationships. That's even true of organisations like Lehman's and Barings (both of which could have been avoided if high-up relationships had been less dysfunctional).
With due respect as a comparative newcomer, that's the biggest challenge I see to NXT - not bitcoin or regulation or distribution or the potential vulnerabilities of PoS vs PoW. It's the flipside of decentralisation but it doesn't have to be as big an issue as I think it has been at times.
I hope it's not out of turn, but if there is one thing I'd say needs doing as a matter of urgency, it's figuring out a way to disagree and continue to work together, or at least in the same direction. (It's not impossible by any stretch - I've got a friend who specialises in teaching businesses how to do it and I'm familiar with some of the material.) If NXT gets that right, they really are going to have a headstart over bitcoin.
Happy to give other thoughts if they're useful, or duck if they're not.
I do feel at times NXT needs a group counseler...
Maybe you can volunteer for this?
We all want the same thing, just the type of people that get things done tend to be pretty headstrong and, well conflict arises, especially across different fields
