Nxt General Discussion / Re: Please Make This Chart : Price To Earnings Ratio for NXT
« on: May 12, 2014, 09:09:21 am »
The problem is that viewing NXT as a traditional investment is a bit difficult due to having no fixed reference. I wrote about this last December and unsolved problem was that any forging gains from NXT is in NXT, and the value of NXT is variable.

A normal stock will generate revenues and (hopefully) profits. The annualized profits can then be used to easily calculate the PE. Divide the stock price by the annualized profits to get the PE. The lower the PE the better the deal,but then again if it is too low, then it might be because nobody things it will do very good. Early stage tech companies can have PE's of hundreds to one, but that is because investors are projecting that the profits will continue to increase dramatically and that will lower the PE to less crazy levels.

So, to map NXT to this model, then NXT having 0.3% to 1% yield per year means a PE of 300 to 100, but this is totally independent of the market price of NXT. So that is not a realistic PE as the price of NXT certainly matters. Everything in cryto is totally connected to BTC and the volatility of BTC affects all the cryptos including NXT. However, most people are more tuned in to fiat pricing

So, when NXT is at 2 cents, then it will represent a much lower PE than when it is at 6 cents. If we had longer term fiat prices for NXT, then we can normalize the PE at the pure yield level when it is at the long term average. Assuming that the NXT price will move toward the long term moving average, then when NXT is below the average we can reduce the PE and when it is above the average, we can boost the PE to compensate.

Of course, this involves a bit of fancy guesswork, but lets use 4 cents as an example of the average NXT price. Then if the price of NXT is 2 cents, the PE will go from 300 down to 150. If the price is 6 cents, the PE goes from 300 to 450.

James
