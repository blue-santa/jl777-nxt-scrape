Sometimes you have to look at Wall Street = these things were figured out 100 years ago...
And remember that 90% of your Customer Base is whales, a few small market makers, and many day traders.

(1)  Small trades were banned on NYSE by inventing the 100 share board lot...
100 shares is about $2,000 to $10,000 on the NYSE depending on stock price...
Any order < 100 shares has no rights... and may or may not be filled on the NYSE or elsewhere maybe.

Say you decree a 100 NXT minimum, people with only 50 NXT will have to buy 100 more... then sell 150 NXT.

(2)  All professional traders on Wall Street pay per/share or per/contract commissions...
Always on a sliding scale that goes down with volume.

(3)  Only amateurs use brokers that have fixed rate commissions... it favors the broker 90% of the time.

(4)  Wall Street also makes mistake forced on it by SEC bureaucrats...
For example, 1/8 spreads in the 90s, 1/16 and $0.05 spreads in the 00s were good for everyone.

Forcing the markets to $0.01 spreads killed Market Maker profits and liquidity and created the Algo Bot industry...
And now you see a reversal where the CFE is back to charging $0.05 spreads on Volatility futures which creates killer liquidity.

For crypto, you create proper spreads by limiting decimal places...
Trading NXT to 7 decimals ONLY... or BTCD to 5 decimals ONLY creates a healthier market... BTER did this and proved this.

And just a general word on "liquidity" (I've made > 2,000,000 trades on NYSE so know a little)...
"Liquidity" cannot be manufactured by one guy like jl777 or a bunch of bots = "faux liquidity"...
Because liquidity matters most at the tails... when the market is booming or crashing... "faux liquidity" disappears.

So true liquidity can be created ONLY in an organic manner where a lot of traders CHOOSE to provide it in one place...
So your real job is to attract 100s of traders to your decentralized markets... not manufacture "faux liquidity"...
Always create incentives to attract active traders and everything else follows (see Bitfinex and Bitstamp)...
