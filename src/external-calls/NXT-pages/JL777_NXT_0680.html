Hi Bernad,

Right now NXT has the most advanced features of any blockchain.  And has many features that NEM doesn't.  In fact there isn't much overlap between NEM and NXT.  When you really examine them, they are very different and NEM has some features that NXT doesn't have. 

NXT is a lot older than NEM and is many ways NEM's older brother.  In a few years when both have grown up more, it will be interesting to see where development is in both platforms and how they have evolved.  I expect both to still get a lot better. 

If you ask me, I think NEM is definitely worth investing in.  Just as NXT broke ground in multiple areas and did things that no other crypto has done, NEM too has broken ground and done things no other crypto has done.
" NEM too has broken ground and done things no other crypto has done." can you tell me more about this? As far as I know NEM has multisig that NXT doesnt have until 1.5, what groundbreaking feature is it having?

Hi James,

Yes, NEM is the first crypto to have multisig with blockchain notifications for users to sign, but there is a lot of other interesting development. 

- first use of Proof-of-Importance (PoI) as a consensus algorithm  (this is the first ever algo that incorporates reputation)  http://cointelegraph.com/news/113698/proof-of-importance-nem-is-going-to-add-reputations-to-the-blockchain

- first use of eigentrust++ in a cryptocurrency for securing the P2P network  (This allows each node to know if the node next to it is being honest and if not, then not listen to it)

- first use of remote (proxy) harvesting (harvesting is our analog of mining).  This allows me to transmit a private key to any node on the network so that I can harvest on that node, even a malicious one with no security risk at all to me.   

- first use of transaction spam protection  (Ripple has transaction spam protection, but NEM has a new technique.  In Ripple spam protection raises the fee price for all people sending on the network.  After the spam stops, the fees for all people go back down.  In NEM the spammers transactions get put in a que and if there really is that much spam, they would eventually time out.  The spammers in NEM can push their transactions through, but they will have to pay more and more fees.  There is also a high fee for people sending low fee transactions.)

- first crypto with an egalitarian distribution to over 1500 individuals. please watch here  http://www.currly.com/s/f87adc3/ A Quantitative Analysis of Altcoins

- also as far as I know NEM is the first ever crypto with p2p time syncing

(All of these firsts are going to allow an interesting platform where new features built can do new things)

In addition NEM differs from NXT in other points. 

NXT you have a passphrase and only one account.  That means if you switch accounts you have to log out and log back in with a different password.  For people that change accounts a lot (like bitcoiners), this is a huge problem.  In NEM you can have 1 wallet with 1000 accounts in it.  One log-in, one password.  This makes some issues with mutisig much easier.  Imagine building a large business on NXT where accounts have lots of different accounts and different multisig accounts are controlled by different people.  These people will be logging in and out A LOT.  In NEM each person just has to log in once.  (Qora, Node, Crypti, and all the other Java 2.0 forks of NXT are all like NXT in that they have one passphrase per one account.)

NEM has variable fees

NEM was designed via test driven development from day one. We believe that will lead to a platform that will be less buggy and quicker and easier to adapt in the future.  NEM devs have actually spent way more time and code writing tests than the actual code for NEM.  I've watched as you have time and time again tried to fix one bug at a time on your code.  Your determination is amazing and it is really interesting because you are making software that is breaking ground.  Still.... I am sure you can appreciate this form of software development, so that each time there is an update, the whole thing won't have to be debugged as much line by line (in theory anyway). 

There might be more, but that is the list of things that come to mind right now. 

In conclusion.... NEM devs are talented.  They are many.  And they are working hard.
It sounds like NEM has spent a lot of effort to reengineering things and this is certainly a fine thing, but the common sense test of whether NEM has created groundbreaking things is what it enables that previously was not possible.

a) multisig, well this is already possible with bitcoin for years
b) eigentrust... this is interesting if it is immune to attack
c) "transmit a private key" this sounds like a really bad idea, I must be misunderstanding. In any case NXT allows to lease forging
d) who decides if it is spam? what it if is just an active account? it is of course better to have spam protection than not, but for normal users, this is not much of an issue
e) egalitarian? even if it was true it is irrelevant as only jealous people care about such things. Also, I have quite a few NEM, so it doesnt seem like everybody the same. I suggest you guys just stop with this angle as it is far from reality, especially once Pareto effects kick in
f) what is wrong with ntp?
g) multilogin is nice, but I can do that just with a hashchain so it doesnt enable anything new
h) variable fees, again nice but most users wont care that much about this
i) theoretically if you have all the test software this is nice, but it means the project will take 2x to 5x longer

It sounds like NEM has engineered a solid crypto with many small improvements, but other than eigentrust I am not having anything jump out to me that is enabled from these things. If the continuous delivery system is in place, then that will hopefully allow the NEM devs to develop at a faster pace some actual ground breaking things.

James

P.S. As far as this PoI thing, I am agnostic toward all this PoW, PoS, PoI and users dont care about this. Most of them dont even know the difference, though a lot of users like PoS currencies as they stake/forge, but it is not because it is not PoW that they like it, they just like the extra coins
