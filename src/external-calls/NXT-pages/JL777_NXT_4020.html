I just want to understand this "nothing at stake"-problem and want to know if that would be a real problem.

The problem with the "nothing at stake" problem is that it's a generic claim about how Proof of Stake is a failure, leveled at ALL PoS coins, that does not account for the fact that PoS implementations are not all the same.

I have yet to see a coherent argument that proves Nxt has "nothing at stake".

I could just as easily label a "nothing at work" claim and level it against Bitcoin.  Verifying a block signature in Bitcoin does not prove that "work" was done; it just proves that a valid block solution was found.  Because it's all based on probability, I don't know if it took 1 hash attempt to solve the block, or several hundred billion.  People just assume it took "some" work, because on the whole the chance of it taking "no work" is extremely low.

And what value is "work" if it's useless work?  I can put my car up on rollers and run the engine all day, but if the wheels just spin and the engine just burns gas and the car actually foes nowhere, is the car useful?  Even in the PoW community, this idea causes divisions; that's why we have Primecoin and Einsteinium and other alts that dedicate at least some of the computing power to solving REAL problems, instead of work-for-the-sake-of-work problems.

Here's the bottom line: the "mining" industry is extremely large and profitable (for those who have the power or have found ways to leverage that power), and like ANY industry it compulsively protects its own existence.  PoS coins are a threat to people who are spending thousands and thousands of dollars in hardware and want to make a return on their investment.  Bitcoin is now faced with the terrible problem of having to restrict its core changes to "ones the mining community can agree with", and whoever Satoshi is... well, he didn't predict this outcome, and I'll bet he knows it's an epic fail.

Quote from: greyw00lf on June 20, 2014, 05:34:17 pm
The attacker wouldn't forge any blocks with such little Nxt. And buying a lot of Nxt for that attack would be dumb.

There's an additional piece that you've probably not thought of.  In order to "pick a fork to forge on" you'd have to be in control of multiple nodes, and you'd have to understand the completely network topology in real time in order to understand which fork those nodes are on.  Moreover, to be effective with your attack you'd also have to be able to put all of your nodes on the same fork and have them successfully forge before the fork is detected and orphaned.  It is not trivial.

In short: how do you predict a fork, pick it, and then put all of your stake on it?

The answer to that question -- if it exists -- addresses the "nothing at stake" problem rather handily.

People who insist that PoS has a "nothing at stake" problem assume it is trivial to plop down a few coins on a couple of nodes, cherry-pick network conditions and ruin everyone's day.  But those people have not actually tried it, and generally have not thought it through.

Nxt does not run any DNS servers.  In fact, you are far more likely to see a DNS outage because of your local ISP than because of the Internet at large: DNS queries are made from YOUR end as a result of trying to access a server, and those queries are almost always routed through your service provider (unless you've opted to use another option, such as Google's DNS servers).  More than likely, your service provider uses multiple, clustered DNS servers in different physical locations.

DNS, as a protocol, is an old (in internet time) and "naive" technology.... but the DNS server network is vast and I cannot recollect a time in recent memory when a massive attack has had a large adverse effect on global DNS.

Quote from: msin on June 20, 2014, 04:13:19 pm
Random questions, but wanted to know how Nxt Services associates with a specific Nxt Account: 

For example, if I open NRS but don't login to an account, then I open Nxt Services for the first time, will it just generate a Nxt account/password for me? 
during test, this is the case, but once we have the enhanced GUI, it will just use the acct you logged into the webGUI with

Quote
Is my BTC deposit address forever tied to that specific Nxt account?  Can I save that deposit address and send BTC anytime to that deposit address without having NxtServices running?
other than forever being too long a time period, you can do what you describe. The issue will only happen if we encounter a change in MGW server hosts. In that rare event, under some even rarer scenarios that I'd rather not divulge, entirely new set of deposit accts will need to be generated. However, we plan for even this contingency and your deposits will be safe, just dont complain if it takes a day to get found and credited

Quote
If I open NRS, login to specific account, then open Nxt Services, does it associate with the account I logged into now (provide a new deposit address, etc)? 
Yes, NXTservices has its "active" account and each NXT account must have a different deposit address

Quote
Is the BTC deposit address the same for everyone, or specific to my account?
each set of MGW triplet servers creates a unique multisig deposit address for each different NXT account

Quote
What if I logout of one Nxt account then login to another account with NxtServices open?
if you are logging/out using different page, then nothing as nothing really happened. It is all an illusion this web GUI
If you do this with NXTservices, then it would change its active acct and depending on whether I have disabled the automatic deposit address generation you might get a new deposit address

I hope I answered all your questions. Very good ones to be basis of FAQ! [doc writers please take note]
