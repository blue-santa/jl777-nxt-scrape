The biggest problem for US players is that the major poker sites have been dark for years now since the DOJ went after them.  So decentralized peer to peer would be extremely attractive.  Many US based pro's pay proxies in the EU to leave a computer on for them and login that way. 

Detecting collusion between accounts would be tougher with pure decentralization so anonymity would not be a viable option.  We would need perhaps random centralized nodes that create checkpoints to monitor activity and make sure the cards are being dealt properly without actually seeing the face value of each card.  Perhaps viewing a hash of the card but perhaps not being able to decipher the face value until after the hand is complete.  That way they can detect collusion after the fact but not potentially violate the games integrity.

The biggest issue online players have with the online sites, Poker Stars and others is the card generation doesn't seem truly random.  We see too many "monster" hands too often.  The cards come out "rigged for action."  Its in the houses interest for new deposits to happen and rebuys; so the house gets paid when they can rake every hand or each time a player enters a tournament.  The cards come out "randomly" in a sense they are not targeting individuals, but the types of cards that come out encourage train wreck hands.  Hands were both sides are going to feel confident of going "all in" and risking everything. "Its a monster hand so I know I am going to win."  A system that players would have 100% confidence, provable, that the cards being dealt are truly random would be a huge selling point. 

I'm an investor in NXT and a poker player but coder I am not.  There is a Nxt Poker project now, think they need help too: https://nxtforum.org/index.php?topic=1631.msg73488#msg73488
