uMGW / Re: MGW improvements with multisig
« on: August 15, 2014, 12:48:02 am »
I figured out a method that should work to implement multisig for NXT, in 4 or 5 steps depending on how you count and I think all but one are not so hard to do.

I have asked kushti for help to get the one part I cant do as it requires creating a special acct to hold the NXT/assets in limbo until it is unlocked. I am good, but I cant figure out how to do this outside the core.

The way it would work is the person that is sending makes a tx with N copies of the key that unlocks the tx. These keys are then sent to N people offblockchain. (I know this is not so convenient, but I work with tools I have and this is something that can be done in weeks not months). The recipients will also receive fullHash and unsigned tx bytes so people can see that the tx was indeed created.

Now it is possible that the wrong keys were sent so it is important to put the assets specified by the tx in limbo (account 0?), that way if someone sends the wrong keys, then it becomes lost, so no incentive to do that in fact big punishment for it as no credit is received when people eventually try to unlock and it doesnt work.

So now we need a "register M of N signers" tx, where the fullHash and list of recipients are specified. Or maybe there can be an anon version where just M and N are specified in addition to the unsigned bytes and fullhash. This will trigger half the tx and put the NXT/assets in limbo until it is unlocked.

In anon mode, it would be unlocked whenever M of the keys are submitted along with fullhash and unsigned bytes. In verification mode, then the keys need to be submitted by M different people from a previously registered account or they could make a token of the fullhash from their acct to prove what acct it came from and all M of these tokens can be put into a single redeem tx.

When properly redeemed, the NXT/assets are sent from the limbo acct to the destination.

For this to be practically used with bigger numbers of N, it would need to be automated, but at least it would be possible to decentralize holding of NXT assets to 254 different accts

James

For anybody that thinks I am just being cranky and dont believe C-f-B

###
http://wiki.apidesign.org/wiki/BackwardCompatibility

BackwardCompatibility is the tool that helps us, software engineers, practice the style of DistributedDevelopment. In the situation when we build our applications from hundreds and hundreds of external libraries, when we cannot control their schedule, yet we need new upgrades of such downstream libraries. BackwardCompatibility is the mantra that allows us reach smooth Upgradability of such components in our systems.

Does software engineering need BackwardCompatibility? It is good for anything else than restricting poor API designers with rules of what they cannot do? Surprising answer to such question is obtained after realizing that incompatibilities cause NP-Complete problems.

...

But imagine glibc developers went mad and published an incompatible version. As that library is used by everyone, there would be quite a lot of NP-complete problems to solve.
###

I feel very strongly about this. I do not like to spend time solving NP-Complete problems that have no need to be solved in the first place. I design very intricate systems and if I cannot rely on stable API, then none of my systems will be stable.

There is no need for debate. We simply cannot just break things
