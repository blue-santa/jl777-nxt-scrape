Coinbase is a shared wallet. So, your coinbase accounts are just a database entry backed by Coinbase's hot and cold wallets. When you pay someone bitcoin from coinbase to coinbase that never hits the block chain. For example, when I buy bitcoin from coinbase that isn't shown on the block chain, under the transaction in my account it says:

This transaction was sent off-blockchain with zero fees.

Here is their terse info about it: https://support.coinbase.com/customer/portal/articles/1392055-why-can-t-i-see-my-transaction-in-the-blockchain-

They tout it for micro transactions too.

When I spend outside of coin base they basically just spent from their hot wallet.

That said, I have done any analysis cause I've never sent TO my coinbase account.. only from it. But, the addresses it sends from aren't always those that are listed for my account. This is why people that spend to "hyip's" from coin base, you know those scams that send you back 4% a day, don't work if you send from coinbase, because the send address isn't always one that will end up in your account if you send back to it.

I think it works this way (off blockchain) if you send to a Coinbase BTC address or to a Coinbase account/email.

I am not caring where the code comes from. I use the best available tech I can find and write the rest. Kind of important to minimize info leaks when you are worried about the stuff I have had to worry about.

Trying to understand the mechanics of BTSX, I put together an example:

Assuming that Mr. L[ong] and Mr. S[hort], have 10 BTSX each, and 1 BSTX is worth 1 USD on the market.

To be clear, the initial balance is:
  L: 10 BTSX (== 10 USD)
  S: 10 BTSX (== 10 USD)

Now, three things happen:
  - L buys 5 bitUSD and lends them to S.
  - 10 BTSX of S get locked as collateral.
  - S sells 5 bitUSD for 5 BTSX

Then, 2 scenarios are possible:

Scenario one: price of BTSX doubles, S wins
S buys back 5 bitUSD for 2.5 USD and returns them to L

Balance:
  L: 5 BTSX + 5 bitUSD (== 7.5 BTSX == 15 USD)
  S: 10 [unlocked] BTSX + 2.5 [gained] BTSX (== 12.5 BTSX == 25 USD)

Scenario two: price of BTSX halves, S loses:
10 BTSX collateral gets unlocked and transferred to L.

Balance:
   L: 15 BTSX (== 7.5 USD)
   S: 5 bitUSD (== 2.5 USD)
